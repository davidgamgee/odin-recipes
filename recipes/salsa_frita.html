Fried Serrano Chili Salsa

Description, courtesy Roberto Santibanez

This Salsa from a family in Oaxaca demonstrates the strong spanish influence on a lot of Mexican food. the grassy flavor and heat of the serrano chiles is very Mexican, but the chiles are fried (a technique that the Spanish brough to the New World) and the mayonnaise-like emulsion you end up with is similar to what the Spanish call aioli (and the French call a√Øoli). Originally pounded painstakingly in a mortar and pestle, tis smooth, almost creamy chile sauce is turned out wonderfully by the blender"

Ingredients

  • Mild olive oil \t 1/4 Cup
  • Fresh Serranos, stemmed \t 5
  • White Onion, coarsely chopped \t 1/2 Cup
  • Water \t 1/4 Cup
  • Garlic Clove, peeled \t 1
  • Kosher salt \t 1/2 tsp

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a small saucepan over medium heat, then add the chilis and fry them, turning them with tongs until they're blistered all over, about 2 minutes. Using Tongs, transfer the chilis to the blender jar
  2. Add the onion to the oil in the same saucepan and cook, stirring until it's slightly softened and golden on the edges, about 3 minutes. Transfer the onion
